# DIGITAL-LITERACY
Digital Literacy Student Ambassador Portfolio 🚀
Welcome to my professional portfolio for the Digital Literacy project. This repository documents my journey in establishing a professional digital footprint, mastering online communication, and promoting cybersecurity awareness as a Student Digital Ambassador.

 Project Modules & Task Reports
 Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic
Folder: task-1-presentation/

Tool Used: Canva

Project Report: I used Canva to design a one-page visual resource to help my peers understand digital literacy. The design defines it as a combination of technical skill and critical thinking. I focused on "Safe Internet Practices" like digital footprint management and recommended tools like VS Code, Notion, and Canva. What I found most interesting was mastering "visual hierarchy"—using design to make complex safety concepts like cybersecurity approachable and easy to read at a glance.

Task 4: Professional Email & Etiquette
Folder: task-4-email-etiquette/

Deliverables: Two professional email drafts and a Social Media Checklist.

Project Report: I researched how poor digital communication—like sending a casual email to a professor without a subject line—can lead to ignored requests. This task taught me that digital etiquette is about ensuring your message is acted upon effectively. By following professional standards, such as clear subject lines and formal sign-offs, I ensure my communication reflects my technical professionalism.
 
  Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness (Phishing)
Folder: task-5-cybercrime/

Focus: Educational Phishing Prevention

Project Report: During my research into Phishing, I was surprised to learn that "Account Verification" lures are the most successful because they exploit a student's fear of losing academic access. I realized scammers "hack" human emotions like urgency and fear. As a result, I am personally committing to using Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on all my accounts. As an Ambassador, I will now encourage my peers to "hover before they click" and verify every urgent email.
